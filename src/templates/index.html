Will Luka Doncic win NBA Rookie of the Year?

{{img.jpg('luka-hero', 'Luka Doncic watercolor illustration')}}

And we've got the numbers to prove it.

By John Hancock | Editor, Data and Interactives

Published Month Date, Year

    When it comes to Luka Doncic, there was never a doubt for the Dallas Mavericks.

    The Mavericks front office had done years of research and scouting on the Slovenian teenager before shipping Trae Young and a protected first round pick in 2019 to Atlanta for Doncic in a draft-night trade.

    Team owner Cuban said getting Doncic in the draft was a "no-brainer," and ESPN analyst Mike Schmidt said Doncic was "the best basketball player in the draft."

    The talk is not all bluster either. Doncic's success is quickly justifying the team's enthusiasim and putting him squarely in the conversation for rookie of the year.

    We rated this season's rookie class in a variety of statistics to determine Doncic's chances at taking home the award, along with who might be his competition.

    Our metric takes into account not just where each player ranks in a given stat, but how much better — or worse — that player is than the average rookie in the class. Those ratings are comined to create two scores, one for traditional stats, such as points, rebounds and assists per game, and one for advanced metrics, such as winshares, usage percentage and player efficiency rating.

    The result: Doncic and Phoenix's Deandre Ayton are the clear favorites for the award, followed closely by Memphis' Jaren Jackson.

    1. He's putting up the numbers

    Relative to the rest of the 2018-19 rookie class, Doncic’s ability to fill up a box score on a nightly basis is fueling rookie of the year speculation for the first-year Maverick.

    Doncic leads the class in scoring at points per game and ranks in the top three in per-game rates for rebounds (), assists () and steals ()

    .

    Doncic’s success isn’t just among his fellow rookies. He leads the Mavericks in points and minutes per game on a squad that includes established veterans in Harrison Barnes and DeAndre Jordan, along with last year’s lottery pick Dennis Smith Jr.

    Doncic’s competition for the award can’t quite say the same. While Ayton is putting up numbers in points () and rebounds (), the Suns are clearly in the tank. Meanwhile, Jackson is one of the better defensive players among the class, but his scoring () and rebounding () are not eye-opening.

    While Doncic outshines the class in more familiar, traditional box score statistics, he's still a standout when the rookies are measured in a sample of the NBA’s advanced metrics. However, just as in our traditional scoring, the real competition is Ayton.

    Ayton leads all rookies in winshare and player efficiency rating by healthy margins, giving him more than a point advantage over Doncic in our advanced scoring metric.

    The Maverics roster boasts more overall talent than the Suns though, leading to win shares being more evenly distributed across the roster. And it's no surprise that Ayton outshines Doncic in player efficiency. As a back-to-the-basket center, the majority of Ayton’s shots come from within 10 feet of the basket, leading to a higher field goal percentage. He also handles the ball less than Doncic, resulting in fewer turnovers.

    Doncic holds his own though, especially in terms of how much he plays and how often he's involved while he's on the floor. He leads all rookies in minutes per game () and is in the top five of usage percentage (), which measures the percentage of plays a player is involved in on offense.

    2. History is on his side

    Of the last ten players to win rookie of the year, nine finished first in either or both of our traditional or advanced scoring systems, and all finished in the top four in both. Only Andrew Wiggins (2014-15) didn’t top his rookie classes in at least one of the two rankings.

    Doncic passes that test, though. He leads all rookies in our traditional metric and is second our advanced behind the Suns’ Ayton. If recent winners are any indication, they both check a very important box

    But what made Wiggins so special so as to take home the award while not topping either of our two metrics? He led all rookies in scoring, and it wasn't close. He scored 554 points more than Nikola Mirotic. And if there's a legit debate between Doncic and Ayton for rookie of the year that would require a figurative tie-breaker, the Mavericks rookie holds that scoring trump card.

    3. He's one of the best rookies of the past decade

    The last 10 years have seen some prolific rookie campaigns, especially when it comes to the easy-on-the-eyes statistic of scoring. Doncic's year is no exception.

    Doncic’s current points per game total of 18.4 is eighth best among all rookies in the last ten years. That's better than six of the past ten rookie of the year winners.

    Going deeper, when we judge that pool of players using our metrics for traditional and advanced stats, Doncic still holds his own.

    Rookie of the Year winners Ben Simmons, Blake Griffin, Tyreke Evans and Karl-Anthony Towns clearly outperform the group of 234 players, but Doncic, Ayton and, to a lesser extent, Jackson are having rookie seasons that put them among a second tier of players just below that upper echelon.

    Doncic ranks 18th among the qualifying players using our advanced metric, and eigth using our traditional. Ayton is 13th in the advanced rankings and 10th in the traditional. Those rankings place both rookies in the top 10 percentile of both metrics.
