NBA Rookie of the Year

What are Mavericks rookie Luka Doncic’s chances of taking home the Rookie of the Year award? By our math:

By John Hancock | Editor, Data and Interactives

Published Month Date, Year

    When it comes to Luka Doncic, there was never a doubt for the Dallas Mavericks.

    The Mavericks front office had done years of research and scouting on the Slovenian teenager before shipping Trae Young and a protected first round pick in 2019 to Atlanta for Doncic in a draft-night trade.

    Team owner Cuban said getting Doncic in the draft was a "no-brainer," and ESPN analyst Mike Schmidt said Doncic was "the best basketball player in the draft."

    The talk is not all bluster either. Doncic's success is quickly justifying the team's enthusiasim and putting him squarely in the conversation for rookie of the year.

    We rated this season's rookie class in a variety of statistics to determine Doncic's chances at taking home the award, along with who might be his competition.

    Our metric takes into account not just where each player ranks in a given stat, but how much better — or worse — that player is than the average rookie in the class. Those ratings are then added together to create two scores, one for traditional stats, such as points, rebounds and assists per game, and one for advanced stats, such as winshares, usage percentage and player efficiency rating.

    The result: Doncic and Phoenix's Deandre Ayton are the clear favorites for the award, followed closely by Memphis' Jaren Jackson.

    In terms of traditional statistics, Doncic has the best chance at taking home the best rookie award. He leads the class in scoring at points per game and fills a box score with rebounds, assists and steals per game and is the best player on a potential playoff team that finished in the bottom five last year.

    Doncic’s competition for the award can’t quite say the same. While Ayton is putting up numbers in points () and rebounds (), the Suns are clearly in the tank. Meanwhile, Jackson plays for a winning team and is one of the better defensive players among the class, but his scoring () and rebounding () are not eye-opening.

    History is on Doncic’s side

    Of the last ten players to win rookie of the year, seven finished first in either or both of our traditional or advanced ranking system, and all finished in the top five in both. Only Derrick Rose (2008-09), Andrew Wiggins (2014-15) and Malcolm Brogdon (2016-17) didn’t top their rookie classes in at least one of the two rankings.

    Doncic ticks that box though. The Mavericks rookie currently leads in the traditional statistical rankings and is third in the advanced rankings. And while rating in the top five against peers in our metric doesn’t guarantee taking home the rookie of the year award, Doncic’s statistical successes positions him prominently in the debate.

    If the past ten seasons are any indication, Doncic is positioned well to win rookie of the year this season. But so are Ayton and Jackson, who both also rank in the top five in both.

    Best in a decade?

    The last 10 years have seen some prolific rookie campaigns, especially when it comes to the easy-on-the-eyes statistic of scoring. Doncic, who leads all rookies this season in points per game, ranks ninth among all rookies since 2008-09.

    Going deeper, when we judge that pool of players using our metrics for traditional and advanced stats, Doncic still holds his own.

    Rookie of the Year winners Kyrie Irving, Ben Simmons, Blake Griffin and Karl-Anthony Towns clearly outperform the group, but Doncic, Ayton and Jackson are having rookie seasons that put them among a second tier of players just below that upper echelon.

    Doncic ranks 12th among the 235 qualifying players using our advanced metric, and 10th using our traditional. Ayton ranks ninth in both lists, while Jackson sits at eight and 17th. No other class has three players in the top 20 of each metric.
